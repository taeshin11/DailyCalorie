4. Accuracy of Calculations

DailyCalorie uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which is widely recognized in the scientific and nutritional community as one of the most accurate methods for estimating Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR). Your TDEE is then estimated by multiplying your BMR by an activity factor corresponding to your reported activity level.

However, please understand that all caloric and macronutrient estimates are approximations. Individual results may vary significantly due to factors including, but not limited to, genetics, metabolic conditions, body composition, hormonal fluctuations, medications, environmental conditions, and the inherent limitations of self-reported data such as activity level.
