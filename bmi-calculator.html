Understanding BMI

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely used screening tool that estimates body fatness based on weight and height. While not perfect, it's a useful starting point for assessing health risks related to weight.

Limitations of BMI

  • Doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat — athletes may show "overweight" BMI
  • Doesn't account for body fat distribution (belly fat is more dangerous)
  • Less accurate for elderly people, pregnant women, and children
  • Different ethnic groups may have different risk thresholds
